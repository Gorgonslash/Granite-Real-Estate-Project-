Is House Construction Worth the Cost?

Posted on December 1st, 2022 by Brady Hill
House Costs Information

Buying a house is one of the most important decisions in life. Its where you find peace, create a family, and well…. spend most of your time. This is why choosing a home can be so stressful and time-consuming. You might be thinking, “This house is too small” or “If only it had an extra bedroom!”. We've all been there even if we were only entertaining the thought of buying a home. Have you ever considered that building a house might be a better option?


Why would someone want to build a house?

Yes, you read that correctly. Building a house may be a suitable option than buying someone else's home. Why would you want to do this? Aside from being able to choose the exact location you want, custom floorplans, personal touches, and designing it to fit you/your family perfectly, you might be surprised to hear that it isn't as expensive as you think!


Isn't building a house more expensive than buying an existing one?

The average cost of building a house in Vermont is around $225 per square foot. The average square footage of a 3-bedroom house is around 1400 square feet. This would add up to around $315,000. According to Zillow, Vermont's current median listing price is $374,414. Why settle when you could get you want for nearly the same price or cheaper? Get in touch with a realtor today!
